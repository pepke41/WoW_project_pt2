Wrath of the Lich King

While the people of Azeroth were worried about the Burning Legion a new threat was arising in Northrend. The Lich King emerged from Icecrown Citadel ready to conquer Azeroth with his army of the Scourge. Players are now able to quest in Northrend and level up to 80.

New Classes

While there are no new races this expansion there is an exciting new class that can be played on any race, the Death Knight. Unlike all characters previously introduced in the game, when starting a Death Knight the player is automatically started at level 58. During the started quests players play under Arthas, The Lich King's rule, only to battle against him and his Scourge to join the Alliance or Horde at the end of the starter quest line. Lore

Who is the Lich King

The original Lich King was Ner'zhul, or a part of him. Ner'zhul was oroginally the leader of the Draenor orcs, but that changed when he encournterd one of the demon lords, Kil'jaeden. Ner'zhul tried to escape from the Burning Legion's hold, but Kil'jaeden did not let him escape and punished him with a fate worse than death. Kil'jaeden servered Ner'zhul's spirit from his body and his soul became the entity known as the Lich King. The Lich King was originally created to be a tool for the Burning Legion.

The New Lich King

Because the Lich King contained Ner'zhul's spirit it had a mind of its own, and eventually broke free of the Legion's control with the purpose of destroying Azeroth. Ner'zhul needed a body for his Lich King soul. Ner'zhul chose a human by the name of Arthas. Arthas ended up over powering Ner'zhul and effectively killing his spirit that remained in the Lich King entity, leaving just Arthas who know blamed complete Azeroth destructin

The Death Knight

Arthas needed an army and used his undead Scourge as that source. The Death Knight class is part of the army. In the early introductory quests players do Arthas's dirty work, killing the people of Northrend and them bringing them back as the Scourge. The players Death Knight after seeing these atrocities however, cannot continue to follow Arthas and so the player defects to help the Horde/Alliance battle against Arthas's army.

What awaits in Northrend

Players will enter Northrend either in the Howling Fjord (Horde) or the Borean Tundra (Alliance). From there they will fight back against the plague that Arthas has created.

Raids

  • The Nexus (The Eye of Eternity): The Nexus is home to many dragons, and contains both dunegons and the final raid. The Eye of Eternity is the last instance in the Nexus and is home to Malygos, the blue dragon aspect. Once a honorable dragon, Malygos went mad with power, and now is bent on destroying the lesser races.
  • Ulduar: Ulduar is the prison of Yogg-Saron, one of the old gods of Azeroth. The old gods wreaked havoc over Azeroth, and therefore he was imprisoned. Yogg-Saron now exposed to the people of Azeroth through Northrend must be stopped before he begins to cause problems again.
  • Naxxramas: The Naxxramas is found floating in Northrend in Dragonblight and is home to one of the Lich King's most powerful officers, Kel'Thuzzad. In order to stop the Lich King his officers must be stopped as well.
  • The Obsidian Sanctum: The Obsidian Sanctum is the secret chamber of the Black Dragon Flight. As some of the other Drangons the Black Dragon Flight believed in the destruction of the lesser races. The Black Dragons must be defeated, before they defeat the people of Azeroth.
  • Icecrown Citadel: Icecrown Citadel is a large structure that is split into 5 locations, the lower spire, the plagueworks, the crimson hall, the frostwing halls, and finally the Frozen Throne. The Frozen Throne is the home of the Lich King. To save Azeroth the brave heroes of Azeroth must defeat him.
